Gender Bias

its cumulative effect on careers and promotions

THE CONCEPT

What is the impact of bias in job promotions?

Can a small amount of gender bias have a measurable impact on a company's hierarchy?

This simulation was inspired by "Male-Female Differences: A Computer Simulation" and illustrates the impact of bias on a simplified dataset.

BASIS FOR THIS SIMULATION

* Level 1 is the lowest employee level. Top scoring employees are promoted to open positions in the next level.

* Each employee level begins with equal number of men and women.

* At each promotional period 15% of employees at all levels are randomly removed. (15% attrition rate)

* 12 promotional cycles (2 per year for 6 years)

* Promotion scores are randomly assigned from 1-100, or 1-100 + bias amount for the 'favored' gender.

Note: This simulation uses random selection for attrition and promotional scores. So the output will have a small variance.
